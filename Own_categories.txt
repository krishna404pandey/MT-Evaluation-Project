There are a total of 17 categories, among them 2 are default categories – ‘Default_similar’ and ‘Default_dissimilar’, and the rest 15 are main categories (5 are in the ‘Similar’ group and 10 are in the ‘Dissimilar’ group).


Similar Translation Categories
Word_synm: Uses synonymous words while preserving the meaning of the reference.
Mixd_lang: Combines source- and target-language words or converting scripts while maintaining equivalence with the reference.
Negt_anto: Preserves meaning through the combined use of negation and antonymic expressions.
Identical: Matches the reference text exactly or with negligible variation.
Fluent: Conveys the same meaning as the reference with minor, fluent variations.
Default_similar: Is semantically similar or a valid paraphrase of the reference through other means.

Dissimilar Translation Categories
Anto_flip: Contradicts the reference meaning due to an antonym substitution.
Negt_flip: Contradicts the reference meaning because of incorrect negation.
Gend_flip: Differs from the reference due to a gender mismatch.
Sing_plul: Differs from the reference due to incorrect singular–plural usage.
Tens_chng: Differs from the reference due to a tense mismatch.
Word_ordr: Differs from the reference due to a word order change that alters meaning.
Word_rplc: Uses semantically dissimilar words in place of reference terms.
Add_extra: Introduces additional content that changes the intended meaning.
Omission: Omits essential content required to preserve full meaning.
Neutral: Is unrelated or semantically disconnected from the reference.
Default_dissimilar: Differs from the reference through other unspecified mechanisms.
